If You've Been Asking These Questions,
You're in the Right Place

"I have old mercury fillings and I've been worried about them for years. Is removal actually safe?"

It depends entirely on how it's done. Improper amalgam removal can actually increase your exposure to mercury vapor. We use the SMART protocol (Safe Mercury Amalgam Removal Technique) — the gold standard established by the IAOMT — with special suction, rubber dams, and protective measures that significantly reduce vapor exposure during removal.

"I need an implant but I don't want titanium in my body. What are my options?"

Ceramic zirconia implants are a well-established, metal-free alternative. Zirconia is non-metallic, non-corrosive, and highly biocompatible — it doesn't conduct electricity or interfere with energy fields the way metal implants can. We are members of the International Academy of Ceramic Implants and place ceramic implants regularly.

"Every dentist I've seen just wants to fill things quickly. I want someone who actually explains what's going on."

We intentionally over-schedule time for every appointment — for consultations and procedures alike. Our patients are health-conscious and curious, and we believe you should understand why we recommend what we recommend. Questions are encouraged. We're not interested in rushing.

"I've read that oral health is connected to overall health but my regular dentist never mentions it."

It's a central part of our philosophy. Oral infections, periodontal disease, and toxic materials in the mouth don't stay local — they have pathways into your lymphatic and circulatory systems. Biological dentistry considers your mouth as part of your whole body, not a separate compartment.

"I'm nervous about dental X-rays. My last office seemed to take them constantly."

We share that concern. We take the fewest X-rays clinically necessary and use equipment calibrated to produce the lowest radiation levels possible for accurate diagnostics. We'll always explain why a specific image is needed and won't take one without a clear reason.

"I want a dentist who treats me like a person, not a procedure."

We opened this practice because we saw what corporate dentistry was doing to patients. Here, your care is never shaped by production targets or quotas. Dr. Ellingson's team is small by design — because a smaller practice means every patient gets the attention they deserve.

The Principles That Guide Every Visit

🌿

Most Biocompatible Materials Available

We research every material we use and will switch when something safer and equally durable becomes available. Zirconia ceramic for restorations and implants. Inert ceramic cements and filling materials that don't leach. Nothing goes in your mouth that we wouldn't be comfortable with ourselves.

🔬

Minimally Invasive — Always

You only have one set of teeth. Our starting position is always: preserve as much natural tooth structure as possible. We use techniques that minimize vibration and heat transfer. We won't recommend a procedure if a less invasive option exists and is appropriate for your situation.

Mouth-Body Connection

Oral health doesn't stay in your mouth. Infections, periodontal disease, and materials in your mouth travel through your lymphatic and circulatory systems. We consider how every treatment decision affects your overall health — not just the tooth in front of us.

Corporate dentistry runs on patient volume. We don't. Every appointment is over-scheduled so no one — patient or clinician — is ever rushed. Consultations are long enough for your questions. Procedures have enough time to be done right the first time.

Common Questions

What Patients Ask
Before Their First Visit

Biological dentistry applies the same core dental skills as conventional dentistry — fillings, crowns, implants, cleanings — but with a different philosophy around materials and approach. Biological dentists prioritize biocompatible materials (ceramics over metals), minimally invasive techniques, and consider the mouth as part of the whole body rather than an isolated system. The goal is the same: a healthy mouth. The methodology is more conservative and materials-conscious.
The safety of amalgam removal depends heavily on the protocol used. Improper removal can temporarily increase a patient's exposure to mercury vapor. The SMART protocol (Safe Mercury Amalgam Removal Technique), established by the IAOMT and used at Swiss Biologic Dentistry, includes protective measures — rubber dams, high-volume suction, protective coverings, and specific drilling patterns — designed to significantly reduce vapor release and patient exposure during removal. We are SMART-certified and follow this protocol for all amalgam removal procedures.
Ceramic zirconia implants are a metal-free alternative to the titanium implants used in conventional dentistry. Zirconia is non-metallic, non-corrosive, and does not conduct electricity or release ions into surrounding tissue the way metal implants can. Ceramic implants have been in clinical use for decades and have a well-documented success rate. They are not appropriate for every clinical situation — factors like bone density, jaw anatomy, and the number of implants needed affect suitability. We'll evaluate your specific situation and discuss all options during your consultation.
